My name is Kimberly Kreiss. I currently live in Washington, D.C. where I’m serving as a Senior Research Assistant in the Consumer and Community Research section of the Division of Consumer and Community Affairs (DCCA) at the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System. I graduated from Rutgers University-New Brunswick with honors in 2017 where I majored in economics and minored in math and English. I’m interested in using data and empirical research to solve public policy problems.

My current research interests include and are not limited to:

  • consumer finance
  • banking regulation and structure
  • non-banking financial institutions
  • urban economics
I am also interested in applying techniques from data science to construct novel datasets and answer interesting questions. Some that I have been most interested in lately are natural language processing techniques, geospatial visualizations, and both supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques.
